The Five Domains

Health isn't one thing.
It's a system.

Most health apps track one domain in isolation. Momentum tracks all five — and more importantly, tracks how they interact. Sleep affects movement. Movement affects mood. Nutrition affects energy. The family system is the context for all of it.

```
01
Movement

Active time — structured and unstructured.

Movement in childhood doesn't require a gym or a coach. Active play, outdoor time, walking to school, dancing in the kitchen — all of it counts, and all of it matters. Momentum logs daily movement at the family level and surfaces what the research actually says about how much and what kind children need at each age.

The platform also tracks when movement involves organized athletics — a specific data point that feeds the research question about whether early sports exposure predicts lifelong physical activity. No pressure either way. Just an honest behavioral record.

02
Sleep

Routines, not just duration.

The research on childhood sleep is unambiguous: consistent bedtime routines predict sleep quality better than duration targets alone. Momentum tracks sleep at the household level — not with a sensor, but with a daily family observation logged through the check-in. Did the kids go down on schedule? How did they wake up? These behavioral signals, logged consistently over weeks, surface patterns no clinical instrument ever sees.

The platform cross-references sleep quality ratings with the next day's movement behavior — because one of the strongest predictors of whether a child moves is how well they slept. That cross-domain link is the kind of insight Momentum is uniquely positioned to generate.

03
Nutrition

Family meals, not calorie counts.

Momentum does not track calories, macros, or food intake. That carries clinical liability and labeling risk — especially for children. What it does track is the behavioral ecology of nutrition: how many meals does your family eat together? Are there structured snack routines? What does the food environment look like at home?

These behavioral markers — not dietary assessments — are what the research shows predict healthy nutrition patterns in childhood. A child who grows up eating regular meals with family is building a relationship with food that lasts far longer than any specific nutrition rule they're taught.

04
Hydration

The simplest habit. The most overlooked.

Consistent hydration in childhood is less about daily ounce targets and more about building the habit of reaching for water before reaching for anything else. Momentum tracks whether families have structured water routines — a water bottle that comes everywhere, a morning glass before breakfast, water at every family meal. These small structural habits, built early and reinforced consistently, are what the research on adult hydration behavior traces back to childhood.

05
Confidence

Body awareness and physical self-efficacy.

This is the domain that most health platforms ignore entirely — and the one that the developmental science says matters most for long-term physical activity adherence. A child who feels capable and comfortable in their body, who experiences physical challenge as something they can meet, who has a positive relationship with effort — that child is building the psychological infrastructure for a lifetime of movement.

Momentum tracks confidence through caregiver observation: does your child initiate physical play? Are they willing to try new physical challenges? Do they talk about their body positively? These qualitative signals, logged through simple 3-point observations, build a picture of physical self-efficacy development that no wearable can capture.

Note: Momentum does not label, categorize, or pathologize children. Confidence observations are non-clinical, non-diagnostic, and used only to surface developmentally appropriate guidance — never to assess or compare.
```

Athletics as a Pathway

Sport is one door into a lifetime of movement. Not the only one.

Organized athletics can be a powerful pathway — to coordination, confidence, community, and sustained physical activity. The research suggests early exposure to sport predicts long-term activity levels. But the type of environment matters enormously: recreational over competitive, mastery-focused over performance-focused, inclusive over selective.

Momentum tracks athletics exposure as a specific research variable — age of first involvement, type of sport, context, and continuation. This is the dataset that Title 4 of the connected doctoral research is designed to analyze. Does early sports exposure predict activity at age 12 and beyond? The platform is built to answer that question.

Families who don't pursue organized sport are not doing anything wrong. Momentum treats athletics as one pathway among many — never as a required feature of a healthy childhood.

The Research

What the science says about how families build health — and what happens when communities don't have what they need.

Two levels. One dataset.

At the family level, the research is clear: a child's long-term relationship with physical activity, nutrition, sleep, and confidence starts at home — built through what caregivers model, what routines families practice together, and how health is experienced in everyday life. At the community level, a different question emerges: why do some communities produce healthy, active families and others don't? Momentum is built to answer both — by giving every family the tools to track their health, and turning that data, across thousands of families, into the community health intelligence the public health system has never had.

What the Research Shows

What we know. What we're learning.

These findings drive how Momentum is built. They also define the research questions the platform is designed to answer — at the family level, and at the community level where individual choices meet structural reality.

```

Movement

Children who see caregivers move are 3× more likely to develop lifelong physical activity habits.

You don't have to be an athlete. You don't need a gym membership. What matters is that your children see you choosing to move — a walk, a stretch, a bike ride. That visibility is the variable. Momentum tracks family movement together so the modeling effect becomes conscious and intentional, not accidental.

Sleep

Consistent bedtime routines predict a child's sleep quality better than duration targets alone.

The research isn't telling you to count hours — it's telling you to build a rhythm. A consistent routine around sleep is what shapes a child's relationship with rest. And when children sleep well, they move more the next day. Momentum connects these dots for your family in real time.

Nutrition

Children who regularly eat meals with their family develop healthier relationships with food — long into adulthood.

Momentum doesn't track calories or macros. It tracks the behavioral ecology of nutrition: are you eating together? Is there structure around meals? That's what the research says matters — not the specific content of the plate, but the habits around food that a family builds over years.

Confidence

Physical self-efficacy — a child's belief in their own body — is the strongest predictor of lifelong activity adherence.

A child who feels capable and comfortable moving is far more likely to keep moving for the rest of their life. Momentum tracks confidence through simple caregiver observations — no clinical instruments, no labels. Just an honest picture of how your child is developing the psychology of an active person.
```

Where This Goes

Family data. Community intelligence. Real change.

Every family that uses Momentum contributes — opt-in — to a dataset the public health system has never had before: real behavioral data, from real families, across real communities. Here is what that builds toward.

01

Community health mapping

When enough families in a city log their activities, patterns emerge at a geographic scale. Which zip codes have the lowest youth movement rates? Where are kids logging the fewest organized sports? Which communities are underserved in healthy activity options? This data doesn't exist anywhere else — Momentum builds it.

02

Identifying where kids fall off

Youth sport dropout peaks between ages 11 and 13. Activity decline through adolescence is well-documented but poorly understood at the behavioral level. Momentum's longitudinal data — from kids who are active participants, not just subjects — captures the exact moment and context when engagement drops. That's the dataset that can answer why.

03

Evidence that directs resources

The ultimate outcome isn't a published paper. It's a city deciding to fund a rec center in a neighborhood where Momentum data showed the highest activity gap. It's a school district adjusting its PE program based on real behavioral data. It's policy shaped by evidence that finally includes the families it's supposed to serve.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We do not share connected health data with advertisers or data brokers. We may share only with trusted infrastructure providers under strict data processing agreements, or when required by law.

All data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). Health API data is stored with the same security standards as all personal data. Access is restricted to authorized personnel only.

The Research

There is a question no one has fully answered yet.

Why do people who genuinely want to exercise consistently still stop?

This isn't about motivation. It's about understanding the real, measurable forces — sleep quality, stress load, physical pain, schedule pressure — that compete with intention every single day. The fitness industry has built a $96 billion business on this gap without ever trying to explain it. Momentum was built to find the answer instead.

The answer requires data that has never existed: objective health output paired with subjective behavioral context, collected longitudinally, at minimal burden to participants. That dataset doesn't exist. Momentum is building it.

The Gap in Existing Data

All the data exists. The context doesn't.

Wearables and health apps have created an unprecedented volume of human movement data. Billions of step counts. Millions of sleep records. Hundreds of millions of logged workouts. The data exists at a scale researchers have never had before.

And yet the question remains unanswered. Because objective output data — what the body did — tells us nothing about why it did or didn't happen. The behavioral context that explains the data has never been systematically collected alongside it.

Momentum is the bridge. By pairing real-time behavioral context (subjective ratings via the 8-second check-in) with the objective health data participants already generate (via connected apps and devices), Momentum creates the first longitudinal behavioral-physiological dataset on exercise adherence.

Research hypothesis

"Exercise dropout is not primarily a motivation problem. It is a schedule, context, and barrier-visibility problem — and it has been invisible because the data to see it has never existed."

The behavioral co-variables that predict adherence — sleep quality, energy state, stress load, pain, mood — have been measured in lab settings. Never in daily life, never at scale, never longitudinally, and never paired with the objective output data that validates them.
